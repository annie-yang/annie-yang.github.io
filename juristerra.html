Juristerra

Company: Creek and River Global is a company based in Japan that creates multiple applications, such as Juristerra.

Goal: In order to have people using our platform, our website will solve user's problem of being able to find a database of lawyers and requesting project managers by giving them a easy-to-use platform.

Role: UX/UI Designer

The goal was to increase the rate of new users purchasing our products via our website. We gathered all our data via user testing, interviewing, analytics, etc. to come to a conclusion that our first-timers are mostly are confused on which product to buy that best suits to them, as well as filtering by brands. We collaborated with developers and stakeholders throughout this project. My key learning on this project was organization, such as goal setting.



My UX Design Approach

üìå There will be no images until the platform is public. But I will talk about my process below:


üëû Empathize

I analyzed Google Analytics to retrieve data on returning customers, location, gender, platforms, new customers, etc. that could help us understand our customer-base. I also created a competitive analysis and tried to understand what they're doing, their goals, etc. Next, we communicated with our customers, writing down every steps the user does to purchase a product. Based on the collected data, we were able to come to a conclusion that our users find it hard to filter a menu and understanding which product is best for them to buy. We then created a journey map, mapping each steps the customer does and analyzing the pain points where the customer was not satisfied when reaching the end goal. After multiple user interviews, we had a meeting with the stakeholders to discuss our analysis and what we should move further on with. After meeting with stakeholders, we discussed timelines with developers and project managers.


üí≠ Define

We collected all our data to find a common pattern that multiple customers had, such as figuring out what to buy . We then created "How Might We" questions to help how we could help the customers based on the pattern.


üìù Ideate

We then brainstormed all the ideas together to see what possible solutions we could come up with. We started scribbling notes, drawing designs, creating post-it notes, etc.


üé® Design

We started designing wireframes and prototyping them out.




Key Learnings

Organization was a major learning in this project, such as goal setting. As there were minor conflicts with developers, such as what would be doable in V1, but we negotiated to meet halfway (only if it did not create a horrible user experience).
