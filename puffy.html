Puffy Delivery

The goal was to redesign the website by creating a new user interface and user experience with its new branding assets. My team and I started doing competitive analysis with other competitions, understand the reasoning, create wireframes, and continuous collaboration with the team.



Company: Mr.NiceGuy owns cannabis dispensaries around California, as well as Puffy Delivery, a cannabis delivery service.

The Problem: Not enough customers are signing up and purchasing products

    Diving in deeper:
  • majority of its users use mobile to access the website
  • new user flow that is easier for first-time customers on homepage and signing up
  • new design that complements the new branding assets

Findings

- Research -

We got Google Analytics data on how many returning customers were there, new customers that created accounts, most used devices to view the website, customer's age and gender, cities most users were from, etc. Based on those data, we were able to create ideas of who the main audience was (e.g. the majority of its audience were mobile users). We took the data and considered that maybe the sign-up process was too long. When people use mobile and if the form is long, it may discourage the user to fill it out, thus leaving without signing up.
We also looked at different competitor websites and figured out how many steps it takes to click on one thing (e.g. signing-up), information architecture, etc. We used Google Spreadsheets to create a competitive analysis of at least 3 different cannabis delivery websites.


- Wireframes -
We created quick sketches to see what our brain has flowing. Once we did the general sketches and ideas of what we thought would be ideal, we then talked about our reasonings behind each element.

Image A: There were 3 different lo-fi wireframes being created with different designs. One of the wireframes had 4 different categories: energy, relaxtion, balance, and relief. Since one of the problems were not having enough customers purchasing products, we created 4 categories that would "guide" the customers into knowing what is a suitable product for them. First-timers may not know what indica, sativa, hybrid, or CBD means, thus using words that are easier to understand would help first-timers.

Image B: The old website sign up process was too long, especially if it was going to be mostly viewed on mobile. (Unfortunately, I could not find the old sign up screen after the website was updated live) But the sign-up form contained a first name field, last name field, email field, password field, phone number field, upload photo ID field, and an external link if the customer has a medical recommendation. Since one of the problems was not enough customers were signing up, we created a much simpler form containing only email field, password field, phone number field, upload ID field with a toggle (if on) that displays medical recommendation field.

Once we talked about our designs, we started narrowing down on navigation architecture. By looking at the old site, we knew we could simplify the information architecture.

Image C: The navigation header consists of Menu, FAQ, Delivery Info, Old site, Contact Us, Sign in button, Register button, and Cart button.
  • Menu takes customer to all the products within the website

  • FAQ takes customer to frequently asked questions page (my thought: what if customers didn't know what FAQ meant? It could mean "Fine Arts Quartet". An alternative could be "Help")

  • Delivery Info takes customer to information and disclaimer page (my thought: this page could be under "Help", thus, organizing the navigation even more).

  • Old site takes customer to product categories page (my thought: I thought it would take me to their old site -- such as if they were doing A/B testing to see how many users actually click on the old site versus the "new" one, it would make sense.)

  • The "Register" and "View Cart" button are both call-to-actions. The goal was to have new customers sign-up, so instead, we made the "View Cart" button an shopping cart icon.

Our redesign of the homepage with the new branding assets:

  • Navigation
    • customers know they are on the homepage with actual link to redirect them back to homepage without tapping logo
    • when customers hover over "Shop", they will get drop down of "Browsing by Brands" or "All Products"
    • when customers hover over "Help", they will get drop down of "Frequently Asked Questions", "Order Tracking", and "Contact Us"
  • Body
    • display only 4 categories so the homepage doesn't take forever to load
    • "Best Sellers", "Flower", "Vape", and "CBD" categories are based off (analytics) of most browsed categories
  • Footer
    • footer matches the same text as navigation header to keep consistency
