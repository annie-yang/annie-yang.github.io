Delightful Natural Food

www.delightfulnaturalfood.com

Too Long; Didn't Read (tl;dr)
My goal was to create a website for Delightful Natural Food. Many consumers go on the website, but information was very limited to what the consumers would want. I simplified the user experience by providing certain features, such as location to where to purchase iced beverages in stores and adding a chatbot.

Company: Joe & Bobby Inc. sells healthy beverages and food to consumers. They only sell their iced beverages in certain stores in the bay area and central valley. Their dry teas and food products are only available to be sold online. They often go to stores in the bay area and central valley to demo some of their iced beverages for people to try, so there is a lot of information that was needed to add to their website for consumers to get the full experience.

The Problem: We noticed some important information were missing from the website. When demoing, consumers would ask us questions regarding the tea that were not listed in the website. So to be more clear, we decided to add more information that users would frequently ask.

    Diving in deeper:
  • when users go to the website, they had no details of why they should and where can they purchase their products
  • large images taking too long to load

Results

- User Research -
I needed to figure out the target audience, so I was able to go to demos where the employees would demo their iced beverages. I was able to talk to some consumers and see who were interested in the iced beverages. After a few rounds of demos, I noticed the majority of the consumers were mostly aged 21 and older. There were kids around the age of 7 that would also try the iced beverages, but the rate was low compared to adults. Aside from going to demos, my next approach was to see how the users were using the website. In order to complete that task, I used Samestate, a tool that tracks user interactions and allows me to see where the consumers are targeting on the website.
*Samestate is no longer active*

    In the video, it appears that the user clicked on the product name, thinking it would lead to the product. But the name was not clickable. When the user clicks on "Add to Cart" button, nothing happens, but it's supposed to open another tab of their shopping cart.

    In the video, it appears that Twitter bootstrap was not displaying at all, creating a not friendly and ugly interface. The user quickly exited the web app because everything was not in order, especially the images showing really large.

After the user's interaction, I used an online tool to generate browser testing in order to make sure the browsers are showing up properly with the associated operating system. User 2 (as listed above) was using a Chrome Browser with Windows OS. It also appears that browser Dillo and Ubuntu OS wasn't showing up properly on our website either. One way to solve this problem was to ensure the HTML & CSS code is cleanly written.



- Personas / Flow -
After compiling my research, I was able to create two personas to provide me a personality and a profile to work with. My target audience was aged from mostly 21 and older based in the mostly bay area. There were also kids aged of 7, but it was rarer. So when creating my personas, I chose an elderly woman a young man. The reason why I chose an elderly woman was that there were more middle-aged to an elderly woman when compared to men. I chose a young man because there were more younger men than women when sampling beverages. After creating the personas, I created user flows to help me understand the process it takes to complete a task. I then created Red Routes to help me prioritize which is more important on any usability of key user journeys.





- Competitive Analysis -
I audited some features from competitor websites and results indicated that Delightful Natural Food was missing certain features that would provide the user a better experience.

Priorities and goals Delightful Natural Food needed based on competitors:
  • Where to purchase beverages in stores
  • Hirarchy of content
  • Image sizes
  • Adding Chatbot
  • FAQ

Key Learnings

Delightful Natural Food had an increase in website traffic and more consumers interacting with the chatbot. Consumers easily have access to the most asked questions on the website.
Prioritizing goals. I shifted some of the ideas, such as ratings and search features into prioritizing what was able to be completed within a range of time and implementation.
