Shapr (Side Project)

https://app.staffany.com

Too Long; Didn't Read (tl;dr)
My goal was to redesign the Shapr iOS app based on App Store and my own experience feedback. I reorganized the content and added some features, such as video chat/call.

Company: Shapr is an iOS & Android app that allows users to expand their professional networks. Their users are ranged from teens to middle-aged users from all different genders.

The Problem: I noticed users are complaining a lot about the user experience when using the app, especially the chat messages.

    Diving in deeper:
  • app takes awhile to load due to trying to find user's current location
  • chat messages scrolling was buggy
  • redesigning onboarding

Role: UX/UI Designer

Tools:

  • Sketch
  • Xtensio
  • Balsamiq Mockups 3
  • Marvel
  • Zeplin

Findings

- User Research -
I went to the app store and looked at the low reviews to see what the consumer's opinions/troubles were when using the app. Based on the reviews, a lot of people had trouble loading the app or getting errors. A few people suggested not adding the timer to the app due to not even being able to find one user to connect with or having the users occur more frequently. (In Shapr, daily, a user gets a certain amount of batches of users (~19 users) and then have to wait 24 hours (the timer) before connecting with the next batch of users).




Once I gathered research from the app store reviews, I looked at the start-up screen of Weave and Shapr. Weave has a cleaner, yet easier understanding of the app. Shapr is a little harder to read the text due to the image on the background. On Shapr, when you slide to the left to the next description on the home screen, I noticed the app name is no longer there, but an icon that can be replaced with something more important.



I looked at the login of both apps and noticed a couple of issues from Shapr. Luckily, Shapr has the option to sign up via email or LinkedIn, whereas Weave only can sign up via LinkedIn. Shapr has a huge whitespace and on the bottom of the app, everything seems too squished together, not giving them breathing space. Imagine an elevator where people are just squished together to the right side of the elevator, whereas there is so much more room on the left side of the elevator. That...that is what it feels like.



I looked at the matching section where I can swipe left to not meet and swipe right to meet. One thing I noticed is that the tags are expanding too much that's taking too much space. Some of the tags can be expanded to three widths instead of two. Another thing I noticed is that the "Meet" button should be "Connect" instead because the users are connecting with each other rather than "meeting". I thought about changing it "Messaging", but from my experiences using the app, not everyone messages everyone. I've used the app for quite awhile, and I haven't actually met someone yet, I've only messaged them. Finally, the app should have a "messaging" icon to give users an opportunity to explain why they should match. What if the other user passes, but you wanted to match? What if it was a good opportunity, but no chances were given? That's why the messaging app icon would be a good idea without the ability to rely on "pass" or "connect" button.



I looked at the conversation screen of both apps and noticed Shapr has three options: View all, Say hello, and Conversations. "View all" seems to be irrelevant since it's only combining "Say hello" and "Conversations". One feature I think Shapr should have is "Expiring". One thing I noticed when using the app, and also as others have told me is that people that have "matched", yet they both don't contact each other. If there is an expiring option like Weave, it would be a lot easier to eliminate those that match but don't want to talk. Another feature they should add is "Reconnect". After the expiring date (48 hours), if you want to reconnect with the user again, this gives the user more opportunities to reconnect after the expired date. If they wish to not reconnect, then they may delete the user and it won't appear again.



One major flaw I saw while using the app was the inability to see what the user wrote back if I write a lot. Another feature they should add is the ability to call/video chat because in our profiles, we have "Favorite Ways to Meet" with options of video call, voice call, coffee, etc.



Another flaw as mentioned from reviews were the loading time of updating location. Everytime I open the app to connect with fresh batches of users, the "updating your location" loading icon always shows. Why does it always need to update my location? Shouldn't once be enough? There is an option to skip the updating, but why do I have to do it every time I go on the app? Kind of redundant, don't you think?

Key Learnings

Killing previous ideas. What I designed in the wireframes was a bit different than what I had in the mockups because ideas kept flowing in my brain, but I had to limit myself to not add so much new stuff to the app. I wanted to keep the original flow of it, but also create something a little better. There will always be changes being made, but as long as I focus on the project's goals.
