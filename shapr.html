Shapr (Side Project)

https://app.staffany.com

Too Long; Didn't Read (tl;dr)
Redesigned the Shapr iOS app, such as restructuring content based on reviews from the App Store and personal experience.

Company: Shapr is an iOS & Android app that allows users to expand their professional networks.

The Problem: Users were complaining a lot about the user experience, such as the chat messages.

    Diving in deeper:
  • app takes awhile to load due to trying to find user's current location
  • chat messages scrolling was buggy
  • redesigning onboarding

Role:

  • UX/UI Developer
Tools:
  • Sketch
  • Xtensio
  • Balsamiq Mockups 3
  • Marvel
  • Zeplin

Findings

- User Research -
Users had trouble loading the app or getting errors based on the App Store reviews. A few people suggested not adding the timer to the app due to not even being able to find one user to connect with or having the users occur more frequently.

Weave has a cleaner, yet easier understanding of the app. Shapr is a little harder to read due to the image on the background. When you slide to the left to the next description, I noticed the app name is no longer there, but an icon that could be replaced with something else that better depicts the app.

Shapr has a huge whitespace and on the bottom of the app, causing everything to clump together, not giving them breathing space.

The tags are expanding too much that's taking too much space, which could be resolved by expanding into three widths instead of two. The "Meet" button should be changed to "Connect" because the users are connecting with each other rather than meeting, since not everyone meets everyone. The app should have a "messaging" icon to give users an opportunity to explain why they should match.

Shapr has three options on the conversation tab: View all, Say hello, and Conversations. "View all" seems to be irrelevant since it's only combining "Say hello" and "Conversations". Shapr should have "Expiring" because users that have "matched" still don't contact each other. They should also add "Reconnect" because after the expiring date (48 hours), if users want to reconnect with the user again, this gives the user more opportunities to reconnect after the expired date. If they wish to not reconnect, then they may delete the user and it won't appear again.

One major flaw I saw while using the app was the inability to see what the user wrote back if I write a lot. Another feature they should add is the ability to call/video chat because in our profiles, we have "Favorite Ways to Meet" with options of video call, voice call, coffee, etc.

Another flaw as mentioned from reviews were the loading time of updating location. Everytime I open the app to connect with fresh batches of users, the "updating your location" loading icon always shows. Why does it always need to update my location? Shouldn't once be enough? There is an option to skip the updating, but why do I have to do it every time I go on the app? Kind of redundant, don't you think?

Key Learnings

Killing previous ideas. What I designed in the wireframes was a bit different than what I had in the mockups because I had to limit myself to not add too much new features. I wanted to keep the original flow of it, but also create something a little different. There will always be changes being made, but I just need to focus on the goal.
